Law Enforcement And GPS Tracking

Published on October 5th, 2022

Nowadays, you can do almost anything with GPS tracking. Risk reduction is a great one, and our customers can use GPS tracking to help law enforcement during or after a safety event! We've had many customers use our GPS tracking to guide law enforcement directly to the location of the stolen vehicles, resulting in not only their vehicle, but also many other vehicles being recovered, and the perpetrators going to jail. The main thing people use GPS tracking for, is to know at any time, where exactly a vehicle is, and to know when it moves.

We’d also like to help with integration. That’s the software you use on computer, phone or tablet, that gets data from the tracker and presents it in a useful, informative way. Fleet Tracking Software

When we talk about risk reduction for your fleet there are really two pretty huge benefits. You can monitor in real-time the actual location of a vehicle that may be having a safety event. Being able to immediately share this data with law-enforcement is a big deal because the chances of being able to bring that crime incident to a good outcome are greatly increased. When the police can deal with it real-time they can do a lot more to help. In the second scenario GPS tracking data is exceedingly helpful to assisting any law-enforcement investigation, as it presents verifiable facts sometimes crucial to prosecution and conviction.

Insurance companies are going to like you for having GPS. Having that data to hand after an accident, theft, robbery or carjacking incident helps establish fault and could mean the difference between an expensive rate hike and just regular old coverage costs. Even if the bad guys managed to find and disable the tracker, you did right by insurance for having a tracker at all.

So, when you invest in GPS tracking you stand to keep your insurance costs low, you can quickly interface with law enforcement as the need arises, and you gain peace of mind for the safety and security of your fleet. With real-time tracking at your fingertips you can get alerts when things aren’t right with a vehicle.
