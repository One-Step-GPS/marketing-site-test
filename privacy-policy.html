When and how we collect data
When you visit our web site or use our products or services, we collect data. Sometimes you provide us with data, sometimes data about you is collected automatically.

We collect your data automatically when you:
Browse any page of our web site, use the chat feature of our web site, request a demo, use our products or services, and when you contact us for support, or when we send emails to you.

You give us your data when you:
Opt-in to marketing messages, use the chat feature of our web site, request a demo, place an order, use our products or services, contact us for support, send us emails, and when we call you or you call us.

You may also choose to give us your data when you engage with us on social media or other online forums, respond to a marketing program or promotion, when you discuss a potential business relationship with us, or when you give us your business card or contact details at an event such as a trade show.

If you give us someone else’s data:
Please only provide another person’s personal data to us if you have their permission. If you later discover that you do not have their permission, please inform us immediately.

We may collect data from other sources
We may supplement your data with data that we receive from third parties, such as through a referral program. If the products or services that you use are offered by a third party, we may receive your data from them. The data that we receive is subject to this policy, but the third party’s privacy policy will control how they use your data.

Types of data we collect
Contact details
Your name, address, telephone number, and email address.

Financial information
We collect all details of our financial transaction when you place an order through our web site. This may include your bank account number, credit card number and CVV, or debit card details that you provide to pay for our products and services.

Data from your users' use of our products and services
Your customers and their street addresses, your users and their home and work addresses, your vehicles and their VIN numbers, device names, telematics device identification numbers, license plate numbers, the routes and places visited by your vehicles and their drivers, computer data from vehicles, miles traveled, time spent in transit and idle, camera recordings, your employees’ and users' names, phone numbers, email addresses and employers, your drivers’ routes, appointments, and business data entered into our products by your employees or users or collected using our devices.

Data that identifies you
Your IP address, login information, browser type and version, time zone setting, browser plug-in types, geolocation information about where you might be, operating system and version.

Data on how you use our web site
Your URL clickstreams (the path you take through our site), products/services viewed, page response times, download errors, how long you stay on our pages, what you do on those pages, how often, and other actions.

What about sensitive data?
We collect precise geolocation data (within 1,750 feet or less) about your vehicles in order to provide our services to you. We do not sell geolocation data or use it for advertising purposes. We do not collect any other “sensitive data” (like racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious/philosophical beliefs, union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, data about your sex life or orientation, or criminal history) except when we have your specific consent, or when the law requires us to. We ask that you not provide us with sensitive data or use our products to process sensitive data.

What about children’s data?
{{ .CompanyConfig.CompanyName }} is primarily a business-to-business service directed to and intended for use only by those who are 18 or over. We do not target {{ .CompanyConfig.CompanyName }} at children, and we do not knowingly collect any personal data from any person under 13 years old.

Operating our products and services
Managing your use of our products and services, login and authentication, remembering your settings, processing payments, hosting and back-end infrastructure.

Enforcing our rights under our Terms and Conditions or any other agreements between us, to otherwise protect our legal rights or those of our employees, customers or others, or to comply with a court order, legal process, or government request that we are legally required to comply with.
