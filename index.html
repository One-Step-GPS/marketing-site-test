Frequently asked questions

How does it work?
A GPS tracking device is installed in a vehicle (or piece of equipment or asset) to gather information, including speed, driving behavior, engine status, idle time, diagnostics, and more. It utilizes GPS satellites to determine where the tracked equipment is at all times. The gathered information is stored and transmitted by the device using cellular networks, using providers like AT&T and Verizon to send the data back to servers in the "cloud."

All data stored on secure servers is processed and delivered to authorized users, fleet managers, office personnel, etc. Because the data is in the "cloud," you can access the information no matter where you are on a computer, tablet, or smartphone

The gathered data is then displayed on a map in near real-time. One Step GPS then offers a host of ways that the data can be processed and output, such as breadcrumb trails of where the vehicle has traveled, how fast it traveled on the way to a location, and how long it was stopped if it idled, and more. All the complexity of gathering the data is done in the background, so you only need to worry about setting up automated reports and alerts of the important and relevant information.
What types of tracking devices do you have?
We have you covered, from fleet vehicles to trailers to yellow steel. We have GPS devices for every application.

One of our best-selling trackers is the Plugin unit. It installs in the diagnostics port of the car, truck, van, with variants for both light-duty and semi-trucks. They can easily be hidden and notifications sent for tampering if that ever happens.

Other options include a wired device that requires electrical connections to be made and hidden within three feet of the connection. These fit many applications from vehicles, ATVs, boats, equipment, trailers and more.

Most of our units, including the plugin and wired ones, get power from the vehicle even when off. You'll never need to charge them, and once installed, you never have to touch them again for years.

Our support can answer more questions about these units and more specialized ones for equipment and trailers.
Do I get alerts if someone tampers with the GPS unit?
Yes. To put it simply, you will know as soon as someone removes the device. We offer many different ways to send alerts: email, text messages, app notifications and reports. You can also send the information to as many people/users as needed to ensure your trackers will always be online.
What is involved in the GPS installation process?
The plugin can be installed in a few minutes. Match the plug on our device to the vehicle. They both have the same shape, and you are set! It is like charging a phone. If it is ever removed, you will be notified right away.
